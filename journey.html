I grew up in a remote village in Punjab, Pakistan, where education was not a typical life path. There was a small public school nearby with almost no resources, yet there was a spark in me. I wanted to rise, not only for myself, but as someone who could show what was possible for a child from that soil.

I completed my 10th grade at the top of my school, the highest achievement anyone from my area had ever earned. That milestone opened the door for me to attend a boarding college for two years. Those years shaped my discipline, taught me the meaning of hard work, and exposed me to the cultural richness of my country. They also strengthened a quiet belief forming inside me that the path forward was mine to create.

Soon after, I earned admission to one of the most competitive computer science programs in Pakistan. This began a new chapter that mixed excitement with self doubt. I did not have the polished educational background many of my peers did. Instead of holding me back, that insecurity became fuel. I worked relentlessly, trying to prove that starting from less does not mean aiming for less. I began topping classes and building confidence.

Then came a dream that changed the trajectory of everything: competing in the ICPC. Sitting in those contests surrounded by some of the brightest minds in the country felt daunting, yet motivating. Step by step, round by round, that dream became real. I earned a gold medal, winning at the national level and representing Pakistan. For someone who began in a village where academic ambition was rare, that moment felt like proof of what resilience and making the most out of limited opportunity can achieve.

Today, the same fire drives me, only the horizon has shifted. My journey has brought me from a village classroom to national competition stages, to industry engineering roles, and now toward the frontier of AI. My goal is to contribute to visual intelligence systems that are fair, robust, and accessible, especially for communities and environments often overlooked in global research.

The journey is still unfolding, but the vision remains clear. I want to stand at the intersection of research and impact, and continue proving that humble beginnings can lead to meaningful breakthroughs.
